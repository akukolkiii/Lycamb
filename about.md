# Lycamb

Once upon a time, a **Lamb** was stolen from its family by a wolf, torn apart, devoured, and left as scattered bones beneath the open sky.
Nights passed. From the dark trees above, their resin tears began to fall, thick and glistening in the moonlight, gathering where the Lamb’s heart had once been.
A **Lycia Moth**, chased to exhaustion by a restless cub, fell into the amber pool. Its wings stilled, its legs trapped, yet it clung to life.
The Moon had watched them both die.
And she pitied them.
So under her gaze, she bound what remained, bone and wing, grief and hunger, into a single being.
They named themselves **Lycamb**.
Under the veil of night, when the Moon reigns and the Sun cannot see, Lycamb walks again, crowned with a lamb’s skull, cloaked in the memory of teeth.
**And it does not forgive.**
For in its veins lingers not innocence, but the echo of the hunt.
*Now, Lycamb hunts in return.*
